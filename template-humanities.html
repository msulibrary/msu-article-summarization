Who's Afraid of Dissent? Addressing Concerns about Undermining Scientific Consensus in Public Policy Developments

Summary: Dissent is the disagreement of the officially held opinion. This article dives into how dissent can be helpful or harmful for the advancement of public policy. The example of dissent this article shows being used again a scientific community is anti-gmo research, though this quickly gets 'attacked' by the community showing it's to create fear, and not being a legitimate research article.

Important Sentences: While we share concerns about the extent to which dissent can have negative consequences, we argue here that targeting dissent as an obstacle to public policy is both misguided and dangerous. Many have argued that allowing and encouraging public avenues for dissent and critical evaluation of scientific research is a necessary condition for promoting the objectivity of scientific communities.
